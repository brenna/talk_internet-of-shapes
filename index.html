SVG

So another newer technique that's been gaining a lot of popularity lately is SVG

Scalable Vector Graphics

svg stands for scalable vector graphics

and a big reason why it's been gaining popularity is that's it's a....

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scalable_Vector_Graphics

... resolution independent format.

So unlike your pixel based jpg and png and gif which loose fidelity when scaled up, svgs shapes are actually definted by a vector path, which means they stay nice and crisp at any resolution

http://24ways.org/

as an example of this, check out the background on 24 ways

pattern.svg @ 3x

pattern.png @ 3x

this background patter is made by tiling one little svg image. i've scaled it up here for you to see

now compare this with the same pattern saved as a png and it starts to get really hairy

so moral of the story is that SVG images are going to look great at any scale, and especially on high-res retina devices

this means it's a great choice for all your high-resolution devices and that included retina displays
